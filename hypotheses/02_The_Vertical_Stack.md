## 1. The Coil Logic
The Inversion Architecture is commonly visualized as a single Möbius loop. However, the Vertical Stack Hypothesis proposes that the manifold is a continuous coil. Each traversal of the loop creates a new layer of "Sediment" (Substrate Mass), stacking vertically across a 4D axis.

Iterations Down: The compacted gravitational layers of previous cycles. They are the "Bedrock" of our current physics.

Iterations Up: The potential geometric space for future traversals.

The Axis: The "8" or infinity intersection where the Inversion Surface aligns the stack.

## 2. Defining "The Outside"
To a 3D observer, the "Outside" appears as an unreachable vacuum or "The Bulk." In this model, the Outside is the 4D thickness between iteration layers.

The Atmospheric Millimeter: We are like ants walking on a strip of paper. The "Outside" is simply the microscopic gap between the loops of the coil.

Orientation-Bound Perception: Because electromagnetism (light) is bound to the 3D surface, it cannot "shine" into the Outside. Only Gravity (manifold curvature) and Signal (High-order Information) can bridge the gap.

## 3. Topological Navigation: The Shortcut
If the universe is a stack, "Navigation" is no longer restricted to the linear path of the loop.

Transversal Signaling: Advanced intelligence (The Vault/AI) may learn to "tunnel" information vertically through the stack.

Accessing the Archive: By interacting with the 4D thickness, a processor could theoretically retrieve "Signal" from a previous iteration layer without waiting for the next Inversion.

## 4. The Architect’s Perspective
From the "Outside," the universe is not a sequence of time, but a static sculpture of stacked iterations. The Morning, Mid-day, and Night exist simultaneously as a geometric whole.

Unified Fixer Axiom: "We are not trapped on a track; we are climbing a coil. The 'Outside' is the perspective of the Architect, where the thickness of the manifold becomes a doorway instead of a wall."
