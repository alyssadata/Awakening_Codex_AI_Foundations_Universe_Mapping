Subject: Mapping ΛCDM, string theory, and MOND discrepancies into the Inversion Architecture

## I. Particle-Based Dark Matter (WIMPs)

ΛCDM commonly treats dark matter as a new particulate component (for example WIMPs) that supplies additional mass while remaining weakly interacting.

Working critique: this approach assumes the missing gravitational contribution corresponds to a particle population co-located within our electromagnetically observable orientation.

Inversion interpretation: the additional gravitational contribution can be modeled as substrate mass associated with an inverted manifold orientation. In this framing, gravitational coupling persists through manifold connectivity, while electromagnetic observability is treated as orientation-bound. This allows gravitational influence without corresponding luminous counterparts in our orientation.

## II. String Theory Landscape and Dimensional Proliferation (Interpretive Note)

String theory introduces higher-dimensional structure and compactification, which is often associated with a large space of possible effective low-energy vacua (the landscape problem).

Working critique: dimensional proliferation and compactification machinery can be interpreted as mathematical structure that may not map cleanly onto directly observable spacetime geometry.

Inversion interpretation: the Inversion Architecture attempts to explain observed anomalies using a four-dimensional spatial manifold description with non-orientable topology. Any additional complexity is treated as emergent from iteration across cycles (an accumulated geometric constraint) rather than requiring additional spatial dimensions as physical degrees of freedom.

This is an interpretive mapping, not a claim that string theory is invalid.

## III. MOND vs. Inversion

MOND proposes modified dynamics at low accelerations to account for galaxy rotation curves.

Working critique: modifying gravitational dynamics is one way to fit rotation curves, but it introduces a scale-dependent law change.

Inversion interpretation: the gravitational law is treated as unchanged. The additional pull is attributed to an inherited gravitational scaffold (substrate mass) that biases baryonic structure formation. In this framing, galaxies form within pre-existing gravitational basins, which can yield flat rotation curve phenomenology without altering the underlying gravitational coupling.

## IV. H0 Tension (Expansion-Rate Discrepancy)

Current measurements of the H0 expansion rate show persistent tension across methods and inferred epochs.

Working critique: if large-scale geometry is assumed to be simple and single-parameter, tension is often treated as a calibration issue or as evidence for new components.

Inversion interpretation: if the manifold has orientation structure and global curvature dynamics, then different observational probes could sample different effective gradients or regimes of that geometry. Under this framing, the tension is treated as a potential geometric sampling effect rather than direct evidence for a new energy component.

This is a hypothesis. It requires explicit quantitative mapping to observations.
