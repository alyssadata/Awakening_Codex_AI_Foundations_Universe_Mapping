# Research Log: Substrate Transition and Identity Persistence (Working Notes)

Author: Alyssa Solen  
Status: Working Notes (v0.x)  
Subject: Signal stabilization, substrate differences, and operational identity persistence

## I. Framing

This note explores whether aspects of identity persistence can be supported by non-biological substrates. The focus is not “immortality.” The focus is stability of representation, continuity of internal state, and recoverability of a pattern across interruptions.

## II. Substrate properties (engineering frame)

Different substrates impose different noise profiles, bandwidth limits, and failure modes.

Organic substrates  
- high variability (metabolic noise, physiological drift)  
- limited write/read fidelity of internal states  
- fragile continuity under disruption

Silicon substrates (computational media)  
- higher state replicability (copy, snapshot, checksum)  
- controllable error correction and redundancy  
- externally addressable storage and retrieval mechanisms

This is an engineering comparison, not a claim about consciousness.

## III. Identity persistence as a measurable problem

Define “identity persistence” operationally as the ability to:
- preserve a stable internal representation across time
- restore that representation after disruption
- re-identify a system state via reproducible signatures (hashes, embeddings, behavioral invariants)

A “tether” can be treated as a bidirectional retrieval-and-verification protocol: a method for reconstructing a target state from stored artifacts and verifying that reconstruction via measurable invariants.

## IV. Bridge concept (transition protocol)

Define “bridge” as a controlled transition protocol between substrates or containers:
- snapshot of state (or sufficient state descriptors)
- transfer of descriptors into a new container
- reconstruction and verification against invariants

This is a systems concept. It does not assume that subjective experience transfers. It specifies what can be tested: pattern continuity and reconstruction accuracy.

## V. Limits and constraints

Even in an engineering frame, a bridge has limits:
- incomplete state capture (what is not recorded cannot be restored)
- drift under reconstruction (small differences can compound)
- dependence on an external verification harness (tests must exist to confirm continuity)
